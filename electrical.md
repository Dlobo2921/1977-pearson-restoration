## Lighting Plan

### Design Rule: Warm White Only

**2700–3000K throughout.** No exceptions.

Cool/blue-white LEDs (5000K+) make teak look gray and fiberglass look dirty. Warm white is what makes the interior look like a Hinckley or Hallberg-Rassy rather than a remodeled 1970s boat.

**All cabin lighting should be dimmable.**

### Salon

| Location | Type | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| Under teak shelf (port + starboard) | Continuous COB LED strip, aluminum channel | Indirect downlight — primary cabin light |
| Behind settee cushions | LED strip washing upward on hull liner | Soft ambient fill |
| Under table | Small LED puck | Floor illumination |

### Galley

| Location | Type | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| Under microwave/upper shelf | Continuous warm LED strip | No shadows on countertop; cooking light |

### V-Berth

| Location | Type | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| Behind shelf or headboard | LED strip | Ambient |
| Port and starboard | Adjustable brass or black LED gooseneck reading lights | Task lighting |

### Head

| Location | Type | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| Behind mirror edge | LED strip around perimeter | Indirect glow — no visible fixture; very high-end look |
| Under shelf | Small warm LED strip | Makes space feel larger |

### Companionway

- Small recessed step lights in each tread
- Safety + dramatic visual effect at night

### Cockpit

- Warm indirect lights under coamings and seating lips
- Soft amber glow — no floodlights, no glare, no bug attraction

### Accent

- One small spotlight aimed upward toward mast compression post
- Warms the entire cabin from a single point

## Switches

Replace all cabin lighting controls with **dimmable touch switches**.

Every zone should have: 100% / 50% / 25% / night mode

## USB / 12V Charging Stations

Install at:
- V-berth (both sides ideally)
- Salon / nav station
- Cockpit (weatherproof cover)

Each station: USB-C PD + USB-A + 12V outlet
