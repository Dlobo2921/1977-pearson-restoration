# 1977 Pearson 323 — Restoration Overview

## The Boat

- **Model:** Pearson 323
- **Year:** 1977
- **Location:** Monroe Harbor, Chicago
- **Owner:** David Lobo / Chicago Captain

## History

Single-owner from new until David's acquisition. The previous owner, a lawyer, kept the boat from 1977 through approximately 2025 — nearly 50 years. He maintained it carefully, avoided the patchwork modifications typical of boats that pass through many hands, and preserved the original joinery, teak, layout, and cabinetry in consistent condition.

## Restoration Philosophy

> "Don't erase 1977. Refine it."

Every improvement should look like Pearson could have built it in 1977 with a bigger budget. The goal is not to make it look new — it is to make it the best possible version of itself.

That means:
- Satin teak, not polyurethane gloss
- Warm indirect LEDs (2700–3000K), not blue-white strips
- Cream or natural fabrics, not trendy gray or navy
- Bronze or stainless hardware, not black powder coat
- Systems upgrades that are invisible from the cabin

## Intended Use

- Sailing instruction / Chicago Captain programming
- Charter use on Lake Michigan
- Personal sailing
- Possible future electric conversion
