## Phase 4 — Teak Refinishing

### Products to Use

**Cleaner + Finish Kit — Interior Teak (confirmed purchase):**
- **Star Brite Teak Care 3 Step Kit** (~$54) — includes Step 1 Cleaner, Step 2 Brightener, and Golden Teak Oil
  - All products formulated to work together; 4.6 stars, most-reviewed reliable option
  - Step 1 (oxalic acid cleaner): strips gray weathering, stains, old finish — scrub with the grain, rinse
  - Step 2 (brightener/neutralizer): restores natural honey color — scrub, rinse, let dry 24 hrs
  - Step 3 (Golden Teak Oil): penetrating oil finish — apply thin, wipe off excess after 20–30 min

**Finish — Exterior Teak (handrails, cockpit trim, companionway):**
- **Cetol Marine Natural Teak** (quart) — UV-inhibited, holds up to weather and sun exposure
- Apply thin coats; exterior teak needs 3 coats for first application, 24 hrs between coats

**Sole / Cabin Floor Teak:**
- Assess condition first — if seams are cracking, do a full teak-and-holly sole evaluation
- Use the same Star Brite kit for cleaning; 1–2 coats of Golden Teak Oil if sound

**Do NOT use on interior teak:** gloss polyurethane, spray lacquer, or cheap generic teak oil — goes tacky, attracts mildew in a closed cabin

### Application Steps

- [ ] Assess all teak surfaces — companionway, interior joinery, handrails, sole, cockpit trim
- [ ] Apply Star Brite Step 1 Cleaner; scrub WITH the grain with soft brush; rinse thoroughly
- [ ] Apply Star Brite Step 2 Brightener; scrub, rinse; allow 24 hrs to dry completely
- [ ] Sand lightly with 220-grit along the grain (interior joinery only — do not sand exterior rails aggressively)
- [ ] Wipe dust with tack cloth or dry microfiber; allow to dry completely
- [ ] **Interior joinery:** apply Golden Teak Oil thin coat; wipe off excess after 20–30 min; allow 6–8 hrs between coats; 2 coats max — do not over-apply or it goes tacky
- [ ] **Exterior teak:** apply Cetol Marine thin coat; allow 24 hrs between coats; 3 coats for first application
- [ ] Document product name, date applied, and number of coats in Restoration Binder

### Notes

- Never apply finish to wet or damp teak — it will blister
- Always work with the grain, never across it
- Interior finish does NOT need UV resistance; exterior finish does
- Schedule annual light sanding (220-grit) + 1 maintenance coat on exterior teak

## Phase 4b — Fiberglass Polish & Gelcoat

The interior and hull gelcoat likely has 49 years of oxidation, water staining, and micro-scratches. After cleaning, assess whether it needs compounding before waxing.

### Products

**If gelcoat is oxidized (chalky, dull, not just dirty):**
- **Meguiar's M49 Marine Heavy Duty Oxidation Remover** — compound first
- Apply by hand or dual-action polisher; wipe off before it hazes fully

**Polish (after compounding, or if gelcoat just needs brightening):**
- **Meguiar's M50 Marine Polish** — fine polish, brings up gloss

**Final Wax / Protectant (all fiberglass surfaces):**
- **Collinite 920 One-Step Cleaner Wax** — long-lasting, handles marine exposure well
- OR **Meguiar's M67 Marine Wax** — easier to buff by hand
- Apply thin coat, allow to haze, buff off with clean microfiber

**Non-skid Deck (if applicable):**
- Do NOT wax non-skid — use **Star Brite Non-Skid Deck Polish with PTFE** instead
- Restores grip and protects without making it slippery

**Bilge:**
- Clean with **Star Brite Bilge Cleaner** or **Thetford Aqua-Clean**
- After clean and dry: paint with **Rust-Oleum Marine Bilge Paint** (gray or white) for long-term odor and moisture resistance

### Steps

- [ ] Interior fiberglass: assess oxidation level after cleaning
- [ ] If oxidized: compound with M49, then polish with M50
- [ ] Wax all interior fiberglass with Collinite 920 or M67; buff out
- [ ] Hull exterior: same process — compound if chalky, polish, wax
- [ ] Non-skid surfaces: Star Brite Non-Skid Deck Polish only (no wax)
- [ ] Bilge: clean, dry fully, paint with bilge paint
