1977 Pearson 323 — Restoration Timeline

Starting point: Weekend of June 21–22, 2026  •  Upholstery materials in hand (foam + all fabrics)

Weekend — Cushion Teardown

June 21–22
Goal: Cushions out, fabric apart, panels labeled and ready to trace Monday
  • Remove ALL cushions — photograph each one in place before pulling it out
  • Label every cushion with masking tape + marker as you remove it: “S1-Back”, “S1-Bottom”, “V-Berth Port”, “V-Berth Stbd”, “QBerth”, etc.
  • Use seam ripper to carefully open every seam — keep panels intact, they ARE your patterns
  • Lay each panel flat, smooth, and label: “S1-Back-FACE”, “S1-Back-BOXING”, etc.
  • Stack by cushion, rubber-band or bag together

Monday — Patterns & Salon Clean

June 23
Morning — Pattern Tracing & Cutting
  • Lay each panel on kraft paper or Tyvek and trace the shape
  • Mark seam allowances (confirm with upholsterer — typically ½”)
  • Label every pattern piece; add grain/nap direction arrows
  • Cut out patterns and test-lay on new fabric before cutting
Afternoon — Salon Deep Clean

Everything is out. Work top to bottom, port to starboard.

  1. 1Vacuum — dry debris, corners, lockers, under all shelf lips
  2. 2Fiberglass — Star Brite solution + Magic Eraser; scrub, wipe down with microfiber
  3. 3Teak joinery — diluted Murphy Oil Soap, soft brush, scrub WITH the grain, wipe DRY immediately — do not let sit wet
  4. 4Headliner — damp microfiber only, no saturating
  5. 5End of day — apply 303 Aerospace Protectant to all fiberglass and vinyl once dry

Tuesday — Head / Bathroom

June 24
  • Photograph hose routing and sizes before removing anything
  • Remove old hoses; mark connection points with tape
  • Measure and cut replacement hoses (Trident #102 sanitation hose or equivalent)
  • Install new 316 SS hose clamps — double-clamp all connections below waterline
  • Clean fiberglass and vinyl — same sequence as salon (vacuum → fiberglass → vinyl → 303)
  • Assess head floor vinyl — replace if lifting/damaged, or treat with 303
  • Leave companionway open overnight to dry

Wednesday — V-Berth & Nav Station

June 25
V-Berth
  • Remove any remaining items from v-berth
  • Clean fiberglass — Star Brite + Magic Eraser
  • Clean any teak trim — Murphy Oil Soap, dry immediately
  • Wipe down all surfaces with 303
  • Check forward hatch seal condition
Navigation Station / Settee Area
  • Lockers — vacuum and wipe all interior surfaces
  • Check all thru-hull valves in this area for free movement

Thursday – Friday — Fabric Cutting

June 26–27

Runs parallel with final interior dry-out.

  • All patterns finalized and confirmed with upholsterer
  • Cut all fabric pieces for salon cushions
  • Cut all fabric pieces for v-berth cushions
  • Cut boxing strips and piping cord covers
  • Organize all cut pieces by cushion in labeled bags

Week of June 30 — Sewing: Salon Cushions

June 30 – July 4
  • Start with the smallest salon cushion (learning piece — mistakes are fine here)
  • Attach piping to face panels
  • Sew face + boxing
  • Install zipper
  • Insert foam
  • After each cushion: trace finished dimensions onto kraft paper; update pattern binder

Week of July 7 — Sewing: V-Berth & Remaining

July 7–11
  • V-berth cushions
  • Quarter berth / nav seat (if applicable)
  • Final cushions installed in fully clean, dry interior

Following Weeks — Systems & Finishing

July – August
  • Teak refinishing — two-part cleaner, light sand, satin finish
  • Lighting installation — LED strips, dimmers, reading lights
  • Stop cable reconnection and adjustment (engine)
  • Rigging inspection (hire rigger)
  • Canvas — dodger and bimini (Chicago Marine Canvas)
  • Full engine service — impeller, oil, filters, zincs
Standing Rules
  • After every phase: close the boat up for a 24-hour dry-out before installing anything new
  • Document every step — photos before and after each area
  • Keep the pattern binder current — trace every finished cushion before moving on to the next
