# Rigging & Sailing Equipment

## Standing Rigging

### Forestay

The forestay runs from the bow fitting to the top of the mast. It does two things:

1. **Holds the mast up** — works in tension against the backstay at the stern; together they keep the mast standing and controlled
2. **Supports the headsail** — the jib or genoa attaches to it (via hank-on fittings or a roller furler extrusion)

If the boat has a roller furler: the sail wraps around an aluminum extrusion (foil) that surrounds the forestay. The furling drum at the base and the swivel at the top allow the sail to be rolled in and out.

## Equipment Found on Board

### Forespar Whisker Pole

A telescoping Forespar whisker pole was found on the boat.

**What it does:** Holds the jib or genoa out to windward when sailing downwind, preventing the sail from collapsing behind the mainsail. This is called wing-on-wing sailing — main on one side, jib on the other.

```
           Jib (held open to windward)
                \
                 \
                  O======================  Whisker Pole
                  |
                 Mast

                    \
                     \
                    Mainsail
```

**How it attaches:**
- Inboard end: clips to a ring or track fitting on the mast
- Outboard end: clips to the jib sheet or the clew of the headsail
- Telescoping length allows adjustment for different headsail sizes

**Value on Lake Michigan:** Makes long downwind runs significantly more comfortable — jib stops flogging, boat tracks straighter, better speed without a spinnaker. Well suited for short-handed sailing.
