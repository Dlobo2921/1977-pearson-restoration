### Cleaning Sequence

1. Vacuum all dry debris from lockers, bilge areas, and carpet
2. Wipe down all fiberglass with Magic Eraser + Star Brite solution
3. Clean teak joinery with diluted Murphy Oil Soap — wipe dry immediately, do not soak
4. Apply 303 to all vinyl surfaces and fiberglass after cleaning
5. Power washer: light spray, wide fan, keep moving — do not dwell on teak or headliner seams
6. Allow full dry-out before closing up (fans or open companionway 24–48 hrs)

## Step 3 — Head / Bathroom

### Vinyl Floor Question

**Should I replace the vinyl in the head?**

Recommendation: Yes, if the existing vinyl is lifting, stained through, or showing substrate damage. It is a small area and an opportunity to match the rest of the restoration.

- Use 1/16" or 1/8" marine-grade vinyl sheet (Altro or similar non-slip marine flooring)
- Cut to template with cardboard first
- Adhere with vinyl flooring contact cement rated for marine/damp environments
- Estimated cost: $30–60 for material + adhesive for a head-sized space

If the existing vinyl is still sound and intact, clean with 303 and defer the replacement.

## Step 4 — Upholstery

### Hired Upholsterer + Learning Track

David is working with a professional upholsterer and purchased a high-end sewing machine to learn every step alongside them.

**Learning sequence:**
1. Start with the smallest, least visible cushion — sacrificial learning piece
2. Document every step: panels, seam allowances, zipper placement, foam wrapping
3. Label every pattern piece (e.g., "S1 Bottom," "V-Berth Port")
4. By cushion 3, the process will start making intuitive sense

**Fabric considerations:**
- Salon: cream / off-white Outdura or equivalent marine-grade
- V-berth: complement the salon — smoke, natural, or warm neutral
- Buy 15–20% more fabric than calculated

**Materials:**
- 1/8" sew foam on visible faces — gives cushions a rounded, professional appearance
- Marine-grade zippers (YKK or equivalent)
- Piping cord (match fabric or contrast)

**After each cushion:**
- Trace finished pattern onto kraft paper or Tyvek
- Label and store in a binder
- Result: you own a complete Pearson 323 cushion pattern set
