1977 Pearson 323 — Filming Plan

chicagocaptain.org  ·  3-Week Documentary Series  ·  7 Episodes
Every day: 3 beats
1 · BEFORE shot (always first)
2 · B-ROLL while you work
3 · AFTER shot (same angle as Before)
Week 1 — Emptying the Boat & Cleaning
Day 1 The Reveal EP 1
45 min filming  ·  Your most important day — treat it like a movie opening
  • □A-Roll: Walk the dock toward the boat (prop phone on dock box, you walk toward camera)
  • □A-Roll: Introduction at the boat — "This is a 1977 Pearson 323. She's never had a second owner."
  • □B-Roll: Slow pan of full exterior from dock level
  • □B-Roll: Every detail — teak, bronze hardware, engine, v-berth (5–10 sec each, shoot everything)
  • □A-Roll: Hold original Pearson brochure + owner's manual to camera
  • □B-Roll: Close-up of existing cushions — wear, color, stitching
  • □A-Roll: Closing — "Over the next three weeks we're restoring everything. Let's go."
Day 2 Inventory — What's in the Boat EP 1
30 min filming  ·  Empty lockers onto the dock, film as you go
  • □B-Roll: Each locker closed, then opening
  • □B-Roll: Contents coming out — old gear, winch handles, lines
  • □A-Roll: React to surprising finds ("This is the original compass — still works")
  • □B-Roll: Top-down shot of everything laid out on dock (stand on dock box, shoot straight down)
Days 3–4 V-Berth Emptying & Inspection EP 2
30 min/day  ·  Film the raw state — don't clean before you shoot
  • □Before: V-berth wide shot from companionway (shoot this FIRST before touching anything)
  • □B-Roll: Lifting cushions out — tight angles on hands
  • □A-Roll: Show the old foam ("This foam is 49 years old — here's why moisture matters…")
  • □B-Roll: Any water damage, stains, or mold — film raw, don't clean first
  • □B-Roll: V-berth floor and bilge area with flashlight
  • □After: Same wide shot — empty v-berth. The contrast is the content.
Days 5–6 V-Berth Cleaning EP 2
30 min/day  ·  Set phone propped in doorway and let it run while you work
  • □B-Roll: Cleaning supplies flat-laid before you start
  • □B-Roll: Phone propped in v-berth entry, record long continuous take while scrubbing
  • □Close-up: Stop mid-scrub — get tight shot showing the dirt/clean line on same surface
  • □Time-lapse: Prop phone, record full cleaning — extract speed-up in DaVinci later. Great engagement.
  • □After: V-berth clean and dry — same angle as Day 3 Before
Days 7–8 Full Cabin Deep Clean, Fore to Aft EP 3
30 min/day  ·  Work section by section: head → main cabin → galley → cockpit
  • □Before: Each section before you touch it — same angle every time
  • □B-Roll: Power washer in action — wide shot + close-up
  • □A-Roll: Mid-clean check-in — show port (done) vs. starboard (not yet)
  • □B-Roll: Dirty water draining off (5 seconds — viewers love this)
  • □Final: Full boat walk-through from same angle as Day 1 opening shot

Mic Setup (Every Day)

  • A-Roll at boat → Samson Q2U clipped to shirt, USB-C into phone
  • Indoor voiceover at home → Blue Yeti on desk, close to mouth
  • Always do a 5-second audio test before first shot of the day
  • Wind on the water? Cup your hand around the mic or use a windsock

File Naming at End of Each Day

Drop footage into:

/data/Pearson323-Documentary/footage/Day01-Reveal/

Name files: D01_001.mp4 · D01_002.mp4 · etc.
This keeps DaVinci Resolve organized automatically.

Week 2 — Cushions In & Teak Staining
Days 9–10 New Cushion Dry Fit EP 4
30 min/day
  • □A-Roll: Show fabric swatches to camera — name each one ("Crypton Bedford Smoke for the face…")
  • □B-Roll: First cushion landing in place — this is money footage
  • □Before/After: Old stained cushion photo → new cushion in same seat
  • □B-Roll: Running hand slowly across new fabric — tactile, deliberate
Days 11–12 All Cushions Installed EP 4
30 min/day
  • □B-Roll: Each cushion going in — don't skip any, 5 sec each
  • □A-Roll: Explain you sewed these yourself (reference Husqvarna Viking)
  • □Final: Wide shot from companionway — all new cushions throughout. Episode ender.
  • □A-Roll: Sit in main cabin, look around. Say something honest. Don't script it.
Days 13–14 Teak Prep & Cleaning EP 5
30 min/day
  • □Before: Every teak surface dry and unworked — rail caps, companionway, cockpit coaming
  • □B-Roll: Sanding or cleaner application — close-up, show the grain
  • □Close-up: Same plank — before side vs. after-cleaning side
  • □A-Roll: "Pearson used teak because…" — history hooks bring search traffic
Days 15–16 Teak Staining — Best Visual EP 5
45 min/day  ·  Your best visual content — go slow with the camera
  • □Close-up: First brush stroke of stain — let viewer see color change in real time. This is your thumbnail shot.
  • □B-Roll: Prop phone on rail, pointed at section you're staining — let it run
  • □Sequence: Before/during/after per section — go section by section, not whole boat at once
  • □Time-lapse: Phone pointed at cockpit coaming staining — best time-lapse of the whole series
  • □A-Roll: Name the product, explain why you chose it (viewers buy what you use)
  • □Final: Stained teak in golden hour light — shoot late afternoon. This is your series thumbnail.
Week 3 — Rigging the Sails
Day 17 Mainsail Prep EP 6
30 min
  • □B-Roll: Mainsail bag on dock — before opening it
  • □A-Roll: "This is a 49-year-old sail" — inspect for repairs, patches, cloth condition while narrating
  • □B-Roll: Sail fully unrolled on the dock — wide shot. Big visual moment.
  • □Close-up: Batten pockets, slugs, clew, tack — 3–5 sec each (label in DaVinci: educational content)
Days 18–19 Rigging the Mainsail EP 6
45 min/day  ·  Be honest on camera — viewers love watching a first-timer figure it out
  • □A-Roll: Before you start — "Today we rig the main. I've never done this on this boat. Here's the plan."
  • □B-Roll: Running slugs into mast track — tight close-up at the mast
  • □B-Roll: Attaching the halyard
  • □B-Roll: First hoist — TWO angles: at the mast looking up AND from dock looking at full boat
  • □A-Roll: React when it's up. Don't script it. Say what you actually feel.
  • □B-Roll: Sail shape and battens once it's drawing in any breeze
Day 20 Genoa / Roller Furler EP 7
45 min
  • □B-Roll: Pulling genoa from its bag
  • □B-Roll: Threading into furler foil or hanking on — show the drum and process
  • □A-Roll: "Here's how a roller furling system works" — you'll get search traffic on this phrase
  • □B-Roll: First unfurl — from cockpit looking forward as the sail rolls out. Thumbnail quality.
Day 21 Final Walk-Through + Outro EP 7
45 min  ·  Pure filming day — no restoration work. This is your finale.
  • □Repeat: Every "Before" angle from Day 1 — same position, same time of day
  • □A-Roll: Full boat walk-around narration — "Three weeks ago this looked like X…"
  • □B-Roll: Every restored surface in sequence — teak → cushions → v-berth → sails up
  • □A-Roll: Standing at the helm — what this boat means, what's next, chicagocaptain.org
  • □Final shot: Boat from the dock, both sails up, golden hour. Hold 20 seconds. Series finale.
