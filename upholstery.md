## Upholstery Learning Plan

1. Start with the smallest, least visible cushion — sacrificial learning piece
2. Document every step: panel layout, seam allowances, zipper placement, boxing strips, corner technique
3. Label every pattern piece (e.g., "S1 Bottom", "S1 Back", "V-Berth Port")
4. After each completed cushion: trace finished pattern onto kraft paper or Tyvek, label, and store in a binder

**Result:** You will own a complete Pearson 323 cushion pattern set.

### Materials Notes

- Use **1/8" sew foam** on visible faces — gives cushions a rounded, professional appearance
- Marine-grade YKK zippers throughout
- Buy piping cord to match or complement fabric
- The CushionRite Dry Fast foam is antimicrobial and drains quickly — correct choice for a boat

## Interior Design Direction

Retain all original teak. Finish with satin varnish or oil/wax — soft glow, not glossy.

Color palette:
- Off-white vinyl for galley and hard-wear surfaces
- Stucco/warm neutral for salon cushions
- Smoke for berth — darker, more restful
- Warm 2700K indirect lighting throughout

Optional additions (future):
- Two removable navy-and-cream rugs on the cabin sole
- Linen-textured off-white or light gray curtains (replace any existing dated fabric)
- Teak-and-holly vinyl or restored cabin sole
